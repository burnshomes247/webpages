🏠 Private Listing Networks (PLN)
10 items
CompassBreakingPolicy
StatedPrivate exclusives protect seller privacy and allow soft-launch testing before MLS exposure.
RealListing acquisition tool — Compass pitches it to ~100% of sellers despite being appropriate for only 1–2%. Agents and broker benefit from double-ending; sellers likely do not.
Source: Jason Oppenheim, Inman, May 2026 · ~97% of Compass private exclusives eventually reach MLS anyway, indicating the "exclusive" phase primarily serves agent recruitment.
Compass / MREDPLN Expansion
StatedMRED dropped NAR membership requirement to expand MLS access and serve more members.
RealRegulatory bypass — removing NAR membership requirement enables Compass to use MRED as backbone of its national PLN rollout without triggering NAR's Clear Cooperation Policy enforcement.
April 2026. The "Compass playbook": rule change + nationwide MLS expansion + Compass as launch partner.
CompassStrategy
StatedPrivate listing network is a consumer service offering sellers flexible marketing options.
RealScale as competitive weapon — network power derives from Compass's size (~35,000+ agents). The PLN only works at scale; smaller brokers cannot replicate it, creating structural competitive moat.
Oppenheim: danger is the downstream policy leverage Compass gains through consolidation, not just listing volume.
NEW
OppenheimNewWarning
StatedCompass consolidation raises competitive concerns about headcount and market share.
RealPolicy leverage is the real threat — Compass is consolidating to shape MLS and industry policy in its favor, at the expense of independent brokers and consumer interests.
Oppenheim, May 2026: "The weight that they have to shape policy is more concerning" than agent headcount alone. Predicts buyers will eventually need a Compass agent to access Compass listings — calls it a future class action.
IndustryDistinction
StatedAll internal listing networks are equivalent and raise similar concerns.
RealScale determines harm — internal networks at small brokerages are data management; networks weaponized at Compass's scale create market-wide exclusion effects. Size is the variable that transforms tool into weapon.

Boero / 1000wattNewBuyer Experience
StatedCompass's "Private Exclusives" section on its website offers buyers access to off-market listings.
RealGating mechanism exposed — clicking "See Private Exclusives" routes buyers to an agent connection page. Fine print: "Listings are only private online. All buyers & agents can access in our offices or by contacting Compass." Translation: the exclusivity is a lead-capture wall, not a genuine inventory barrier. Buyer with their own agent still cannot access listings without going through a Compass touchpoint.
Boero, "Friday Flash: In Their Shoes," 2026. First-person walkthrough of the buyer experience on Compass.com. Boero's framing: buyers "get the message" — and it makes them angry. Consumer backlash risk grows as Compass scales.
Washington / Multi-StateRegulationWatch
StatedWashington state mandatory full public marketing law takes effect June 2026. Now joined by a wave of states: CT signed into law (Oct 1, 2026 effective); NY passed both chambers (awaiting Gov. Hochul signature); WI, IL, HI bills also active.
RealLegislative front opening nationally — what began as isolated state experiments is becoming a coordinated multi-state regulatory wave. Six states now have active PLN-limiting legislation. State law carries license consequences, not just MLS board fines — a materially higher enforcement ceiling than NAR's CCP has achieved.
WA effective June 2026. CT effective Oct 1, 2026. NY awaiting Hochul signature as of June 2026. Bills active in WI, IL, HI. Pattern: bills require public marketing within 1 day of listing agreement, with seller opt-out requiring a state-written disclosure form spelling out risks of private marketing.
NEW
New York / HochulNewLegislation
StatedNew York "Fair and Transparent Real Estate Listings Act" (S10274/A10679): requires public marketing within 1 calendar day of listing agreement signing; sellers may opt out only after signing a state-written disclosure form that line-by-line explains the risks of withholding from public marketing.
RealThe opt-out form as counter-pitch — the bill's most strategically significant mechanism is not the 1-day mandate but the state-authored disclosure that agents must hand sellers during any PLN pitch. It functions as a regulatory counter-argument embedded in the transaction, directly undermining the Compass three-phase marketing pitch at the kitchen table. The "concurrent marketing" requirement erases the head start: no quiet phase if public marketing must happen on Day 1.
Assembly passed May 29, Senate passed June 1, 2026 — now on Gov. Hochul's desk. Sponsored by Sen. Nathalia Fernandez and Assemblywoman Michaelle Solages. Inman (Darryl Davis), June 4, 2026: "The quiet listing was always borrowing against the open market. New York is just calling the loan."

Northwest MLS / CompassNewData Point
StatedCompass's three-phase marketing strategy (Private Exclusive → Coming Soon → Public) gives sellers a soft-launch advantage: test price, build buzz, avoid days-on-market accumulation.
RealNorthwest MLS, citing Compass's own marketing materials in its counterclaims, found Compass's private phases fail to sell approximately 95% of the time before listings are pushed to the MLS. The "head start" almost never closes a deal — the private phase is a listing acquisition tool, not a selling mechanism. Corroborates Oppenheim's 97% figure from a separate source.
Northwest MLS counterclaims, April 2026. Inman (Darryl Davis), June 4, 2026. Compass's own marketing materials used as evidence against it — a significant evidentiary liability in litigation and legislative contexts.

Compass / DC MarketNewData Point
StatedCompass double-ending (keeping both buyer and seller sides of a transaction in-house) is a natural byproduct of large brokerage scale, not a strategy engineered through private listing networks.
RealConsumer Policy Center analysis found Compass double-ending at 41% in Washington, D.C. — against a historical industry range of 3–12%. A nearly 4x–14x elevation above baseline is not organic scale; it is structural inventory control converting PLN access into captured commission revenue. NY bill's legislative findings section was reportedly drafted with this data in view.
Consumer Policy Center report, covered by Inman April 2026. Washington D.C. market. Note: earlier Inman coverage attributed this to Consumer Federation of America — corrected to Consumer Policy Center. Key legislative ammunition: a number that jumps this sharply catches lawmakers' attention.
⚖️ Zillow–Compass Litigation
9 items
ZillowFederal LawsuitBreaking
StatedZillow filed federal antitrust suit to protect consumer access to listing data and preserve competitive marketplace.
RealPortal survival play — MRED cut Zillow's feed, causing 60%+ of Chicago listings and 50%+ across MRED's full coverage area (IL, IA, IN, MI, MO, WI) to vanish from Zillow. Zillow's lead-gen business collapses without comprehensive inventory. Lawsuit is existential defense of the portal model.
Filed May 2026, U.S. District Court, Northern Illinois. Defendants: Compass and MRED. Claims: per se unlawful boycott and monopoly enforcement. Immediate trigger: MRED cut feed after Zillow refused to display 9 specific Compass listings (from FL, GA, CA) that were publicly marketed as private exclusives before MLS submission — a disproportionate response that reveals the real stakes.
MRED / JudgeCourt OrderActive
Stated—
RealJudge ordered MRED to restore Zillow's full access to all Chicagoland listings — preliminary injunction victory for Zillow. Signals court skepticism of the feed-cut strategy as competitive tool. Court also blocked Zillow from enforcing its private listings policy in any zip code where MRED was active between April 2025 and April 23, 2026, and ordered Zillow to display a handful of previously banned listings.
May 26, 2026. Active development. MRED compliance posture pending.

📱 Portal Competition & Lead Gen
5 items
ZillowBusiness Model
StatedZillow connects buyers and sellers with real estate professionals through its marketplace.
RealLead monetization at scale — Zillow's revenue depends on comprehensive listing inventory driving buyer traffic, which is then monetized as leads sold back to agents. Compass feed removal directly attacks this flywheel.
ZillowContact Form
StatedContact form on Zillow listings connects buyers with the listing agent for convenience.
RealLead capture mechanism — contact form fuses listing display with agent selection, routing buyer to Zillow's paid agent network rather than necessarily the listing agent. Poorly serves buyers who may not realize they're being directed to a lead-purchased agent.
Boero's "plain language disclosure test" would expose this: does the buyer know what's happening when they click "Contact"?
ZillowReferral Fees
StatedZillow's agent referral program connects consumers with local agents.
RealUndisclosed referral fees — Zillow sued for failing to disclose referral fees paid by agents to Zillow, creating a hidden financial relationship that influences which agents buyers are connected to.
Zillow / Realtor.comAlliance
StatedZillow Preview / Realtor.com pre-marketing partnership expands listing visibility for sellers.
RealDefensive inventory aggregation — competing portals temporarily cooperating to counter Compass's private listing network. Portal inventory access is zero-sum; partnership signals threat level.
May 5, 2026. Errol Samuelson (Zillow) key architect.
IndustryAI Shift
StatedAI tools are transforming how buyers search for homes and connect with agents.
RealStructural decoupling — 27% of 2025 buyers used AI to find agents, unbundling agent selection from listing display. This structurally undermines portal lead-gen moats over time. Portals' core value proposition (listing display → agent leads) weakens as buyers find agents independently.
Long-run structural threat to both Zillow and Compass's agent-lead models. Oppenheim dissents: AI impact minimal in luxury market.

Boero, "Friday Flash: In Their Shoes." Important nuance — Boero is not anti-broker-data-rights or anti-double-ending. He is anti-consumer-confusion and anti-scale-weaponization. Directly relevant to evaluating his other analytical frameworks.

Key Terms & Industry Concepts
PLN
Private Listing Network
A system that allows listings to circulate within a brokerage or controlled network before (or instead of) MLS publication. Compass operates the largest PLN in the US. At scale, creates inventory exclusion effects that smaller brokerages cannot replicate.
CCP
Clear Cooperation Policy
NAR rule requiring listings to be submitted to the MLS within 1 business day of public marketing. The primary rule Compass's PLN strategy engineers around. MRED dropping NAR membership requirement was designed to bypass CCP enforcement.
Double-Ending
Double-Ending / Dual Agency
When the same agent (or same brokerage) represents both buyer and seller in a transaction, collecting both sides of the commission. Private exclusives circulating within a brokerage's network create structural opportunity for double-ending.
MLS Grid
MLS Grid
Data distribution infrastructure layer sitting between MLSs and portals. Issued compliance warning to Zillow with deadline aligned with Compass's May 8 feed cut — raised questions about coordinated pressure on the portal.
LAS
Listing Access Standards (LAS)
Zillow's portal-level enforcement policy: bans listings from Zillow if they were publicly marketed before reaching the MLS. Enacted in response to the growing PLN wave driven largely by Compass. The 9-listing dispute that triggered MRED's feed cut involved Compass listings from FL, GA, and CA that started as private exclusives before MLS submission. MRED frames LAS as Zillow "discriminating against lawful listings."
MRED
Midwest Real Estate Data
Chicagoland's regional MLS. Rebecca Jensen is CEO. Named co-defendant in Zillow's federal antitrust suit. Dropped NAR membership requirement April 2026 — the operational mechanism enabling Compass's national PLN expansion. Subject to federal court injunction.
Compass Playbook
The "Compass Playbook" Pattern
Rule change + nationwide MLS expansion + Compass as launch partner. Identified pattern in how Compass has structured its PLN infrastructure expansion: find an MLS willing to modify its rules (drop NAR requirement), use that MLS as the backbone, then roll out nationally.
Hall of Mirrors
Hall of Rhetorical Mirrors (Boero)
Boero's diagnosis of the Compass–Zillow consumer-welfare debate. When competing parties simultaneously invoke consumer welfare for opposing positions, consumer-welfare framing is a rhetorical tell, not a genuine principle.
Plain Language Test
Boero's Plain Language Disclosure Test
Proposed benchmark: can a disclosure be read aloud to a client and have them understand what's actually happening? Applied to contact forms, referral arrangements, and PLN pitches — most current industry disclosures fail this test.
Realtracs
Realtracs
Nashville-area MLS serving ~18,000 members across Tennessee, Kentucky, Alabama, and Georgia. Second major MLS (after MRED) to threaten cutting Zillow's listing feed, May 27, 2026, with June 1 deadline. Zillow identifies it as part of a coordinated, Compass-initiated national campaign.
Measure ULA
Measure ULA (LA Mansion Tax)
Los Angeles transfer tax on properties sold above $5.3M. Has reduced transactions in that tier from ~65/month to under 30/month. Oppenheim: "It's been brutal." Market-specific regulatory impact on luxury segment.
Three-Phase Strategy
Three-Phase Marketing Strategy (Compass)
Compass's structured listing rollout: Phase 1 — Private Exclusive (within Compass network only); Phase 2 — Compass Coming Soon (broader but still pre-MLS); Phase 3 — public MLS launch. Marketed as a "soft launch" that lets sellers test price and build buzz before official exposure. Northwest MLS counterclaims, citing Compass's own materials, found the private phases fail to sell approximately 95% of listings before they reach MLS — indicating the phases serve listing acquisition over seller outcomes. Directly targeted by New York's "Fair and Transparent Real Estate Listings Act," which requires concurrent public marketing from Day 1.

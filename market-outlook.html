Reasons to Sell Now

  • 📉
    Inventory remains historically low Fewer competing listings means your home commands more attention and stronger offers. Sellers are still operating in a supply-constrained environment.
  • 💰
    Prices are near cyclical highs NoVA and close-in DC suburbs are up ~4% YoY. Waiting for a higher price risks giving back gains if rates drop and inventory surges simultaneously.
  • ⚡
    Motivated, qualified buyers in the market Buyers who have been waiting are ready to act quickly on well-priced homes. Multiple-offer situations are still common in desirable submarkets.
  • 🏛️
    Federal workforce uncertainty creating urgency DOGE and federal agency restructuring are prompting some government employees to sell before potential relocation or income disruption. This creates near-term motivated buyer demand from peers also repositioning. Watch list: could flip to headwind if job losses accelerate

Reasons to Wait

  • 📊
    Rate relief could expand the buyer pool significantly If 30-year rates drop from ~6.8% to the mid-5s, millions of sidelined buyers re-enter the market. A seller who waits could benefit from a larger, more competitive buyer pool.
  • 🔨
    Pre-sale improvements could increase net proceeds Strategic updates — fresh paint, kitchen refresh, landscaping — can yield 3–5x ROI. A seller with time to prepare may net more than one who lists quickly.
  • 🗓️
    Spring 2026 inventory spike not yet materialized If new construction deliveries or job-loss listings flood the market, competition increases. Waiting to gauge inventory levels in Q1 may be prudent.

Wild Cards & Watch List

  • 🏢
    Federal workforce reductions (DOGE) The DC metro is uniquely exposed to federal employment. Sustained layoffs could dampen demand in close-in VA/MD suburbs. Scale and pace of cuts remain the key variable.
  • 🌐
    Tariff-driven construction cost inflation New tariffs on lumber, steel, and appliances are slowing new construction starts, which supports existing home prices — but also raises renovation costs for sellers preparing to list.
  • 🏦
    Fed policy trajectory The Fed has signaled caution on rate cuts in 2025. Timing of any cuts will directly affect buyer purchasing power and demand intensity.
